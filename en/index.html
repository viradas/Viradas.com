VIRADAS Method

VIRADAS is a method of orientation and accompaniment for change, developed to work with the experience of pressure in relationships and everyday life.

It helps recognize where relationships, roles, or habits have become tight or suffocating and to create the conditions to recover pleasure and space, without forcing, but with gentle firmness.

VIRADAS is based on a guiding reference structure used to (re)read situations, reduce pressure, and regain room for choice.

The work unfolds through seven stages of awareness and change, which can be explored through different access points (pathways): dialogue, listening to the body, movement practice, and experiential workshops.

Approach

The VIRADAS method integrates tools for listening and awareness inspired by Gestalt psychology, motivational interviewing, Nonviolent Communication (NVC), and references from traditional Chinese medicine.

It operates on the level of prevention, support, and development of personal resources and does not replace medical or psychotherapeutic paths.

How Viradas was born

Viradas was born from encounters with people and life moments marked by the feeling of living in a restricted space, among roles, expectations, or contexts perceived as having no way out.

This experience of constraint, sometimes described in psychology as entrapment, when prolonged over time can compromise emotional and relational well-being.

Pressures may be external, linked to demanding life conditions or contexts that concretely limit freedom. At other times they are internal: rooted beliefs, fears that guide choices, reaction patterns built over time.

Through work experiences in different contexts — from banking to the Brazilian comunidade, from employment reintegration programs to the penal institution — it became clear that people do not remain stuck due to lack of will, but because when inner space narrows, change becomes difficult, if not impossible.

When even a small degree of inner freedom is recovered, even in difficult contexts, it becomes possible again to choose, to take responsibility, and to rebuild dignity.

Viradas arises from this awareness: in Portuguese, virar means “to turn, to change direction.” The term also recalls vir (from Latin, “human being”) and vis (from Latin, “force”), understood as the energy that is activated when body, mind, and emotions return to dialogue, allowing rooting, reorganization, and openness to new possibilities.

The symbol

The symbol tells the story of a turning point—a change that doesn’t destroy, but reorganizes what exists to allow us to blossom.
• The Central Figure: Represents the conscious presence at the heart of the journey. It is not defined by rigid roles, but is a living presence that maintains solid roots while opening itself, with soft boundaries, toward the outside world.
• The Polarities (the lateral points): Represent the opposing forces within us (such as desire and fear, or duty and pleasure). Freedom is not born by eliminating one side, but by integrating them into a harmonious dialogue.
• The Metamorphosis (organic shapes): Lines that once might have seemed like bars or limits are transformed. They become nourishing roots, growing leaves, and finally wings to fly. It is the transition from constriction to expansion.
• The Roots of Movement (the two "V"s): At the base, we find Vir (the human being) and Vis (the vital force). They indicate that every true turning point starts from below, from the body and feeling, to rise toward a broader awareness.
• The Safe Space (the circle): This is the safe space—a protective yet permeable skin that allows one to change without getting lost.

Counseling sessions

Virada Relationship

This is the working space dedicated to dialogue and to re-reading life situations that generate pressure.
Through counseling, relationships, roles and personal dynamics are explored in order to better understand what is happening and regain a margin of choice.

Who it is for
For those who feel the need to:
- clarify a relational situation
- understand dynamics that generate tension
- redefine boundaries and directions
- face moments of change.

How it works
Sessions take place individually, either in person or online.
Through dialogue and counseling tools, situations of pressure are explored, also using the structure of the seven steps of the VIRADAS method as guidance in the process of change.

Duration
Session length: 60 minutes
Average duration of the process: 3–10 sessions, to be agreed depending on the type of support needed (orientation, accompaniment, etc.).

Counseling

Counseling is professional support that helps you understand what you are experiencing, recognize emotions and blocks, and regain clarity, direction, and the ability to choose.
Depending on the reference approach, it works with thoughts, emotions, body, and actions.
It is for people going through standstills, crises, or change, without the need for a medical or psychotherapeutic intervention.
It is not psychotherapy, it does not provide diagnoses, and it does not replace healthcare.
It takes place in a safe and confidential space, with shared goals and timing, to support concrete steps of change.

Those who hold the title of Counsellor SGfB are recognized members of the Swiss Association for Counselling and work in accordance with its quality standards and the professional code of ethics.

Differences between Counseling, Psychotherapy, Psychology, Coaching

Counseling can complement other paths when you need a space for integration and clarity.
Knowing the differences between approaches helps you better understand what kind of support you may need.
The table below offers an orientation summary.

Consultations on habits and lifestyle

Virada Body&Sense

A pathway that helps connect what happens in everyday life with the signals of the body, in order to better understand how habits, stress, and changes influence our balance.
The approach integrates counseling tools with some educational references to naturopathy and traditional Chinese medicine, with a focus on awareness and orientation toward wellbeing.

Who it is for
For those who feel that the body is giving signals that deserve to be listened to, or who wish to create greater balance in their daily habits.
It may be useful, after medical causes have been excluded, in the presence of recurring situations or signals such as:
- fatigue or fluctuating energy
- bodily tension or skin manifestations
- digestion influenced by stress
- emotional eating
- difficulty maintaining self-care habits
- periods of overload or prolonged stress

How it works
Sessions take place individually, either in person or online.
Through dialogue and motivational interviewing, habits, life rhythm, and body signals are explored in order to build together a realistic and sustainable wellbeing plan.

Note on the approach
This work does not have therapeutic purposes and does not fall within the field of healthcare complementary medicine.
Some principles from naturopathy and traditional Chinese medicine are used as symbolic and orienting interpretative keys, helping to make the relationships between body signals, stress, habits, and personal balance more understandable.

Duration
First meeting: 90 minutes + 30-45 minutes for feedback.
Follow-up sessions: 60 minutes.
Average duration of the pathway: 2-4 sessions, to be agreed depending on individual needs.

Yoga sessions

Virada Movimento

It is a space for working through the body and movement.
Simple yoga practices, accessible to everyone, allow people to experience directly in the body what is also explored in the VIRADAS method through dialogue and reflection: creating space, regulating pressure, and restoring balance.
The sessions integrate asana (postures), pranayama (breathwork), and moments of meditation, with the intention of fostering space and stability in both body and mind.

Who it is for
For those who wish to work through bodily experience in order to:
- reduce tension and pressure
- find more space in the body and in the breath
- develop greater body awareness
- accompany moments of change in life.
These practices may also be useful for those who wish to integrate counseling work with a direct bodily experience.

How it works
Sessions can be individual or in groups.
Each practice is designed in relation to the person or the group, taking into account the needs of the moment.
A session may include:
- centering and initial meditation
- breathing practices (pranayama)
- sequences of asana
- moments of integration and relaxation.
The practices may be oriented toward specific themes or toward a working intention (sankalpa), with the aim of creating space in the body and fostering a new quality of presence.

Duration
Single session, individual or group: 60 minutes
Thematic group sessions: 2–3 hours
Average duration of the process: to be agreed according to the need.

Workshops and Training

Virada Lab

A space for group reflection and experimentation where the VIRADAS method maps become practical tools for personal and team growth.



Who it is for

For individuals and organizations wishing to work on conflict and burnout prevention by exploring the following topics:

  • Communication: from communication styles to Nonviolent Communication (NVC), to train in managing conflict, aggression, and assertiveness.
  • Character Maps: from psychological enneatypes to the 4 personality colors, as compasses to understand and manage automatic reactions in oneself and others.
  • Pleasure, Needs, and Values: insights based on Gestalt and other approaches to recognize how we move through different areas of life and what truly motivates us.
  • Relationships and Boundaries: exploration of roles and the boundaries necessary for well-being in personal and professional dynamics.

How it works
Sessions are held in groups, preferably in person, but also online. Workshops may include training moments, guided exercises, experiential practices, reflection spaces, and real-life applications.



Duration
Workshops can range from 120 minutes to full-day sessions. Organizational programs are custom-designed, defining duration and format together.

Marlene Masino


She accompanies individuals and groups through processes of change, through counseling and training.
Her work arises from encounters with situations in which roles, relationships, or life contexts generate pressure and reduce room for choice.


Mission, vision and values

Mission
To contribute to people’s well-being by supporting those who feel “in a cage” to recognize who they are, what they want, and what concrete steps they can take to live choices and relationships with greater pleasure and satisfaction.

Vision
Respectful relationships and life contexts where it is possible to move without constraints and destructive dynamics, staying true to oneself in one’s choices.

Values

  • freedom of choice and movement
  • pleasure and well-being in relationships
  • clear communication and boundaries

Professional experience

After her first years after university in online marketing in the banking sector, Marlene also changed course.
Training in counseling and a volunteer experience in 2009 oriented her work toward the helping relationship.

Since then, she has gained experience in contexts where choices are limited and change becomes necessary:

  • with adolescents in contexts of marginalization, poverty and violence in Brazil, for three months.

The counseling offered through Viradas falls within the field of the helping relationship, prevention, and personal growth.

It is not psychotherapy, it is not a healthcare profession, it does not provide diagnoses, it does not perform medical or psychological treatments, and it does not replace therapeutic or psychiatric pathways.

The service is intended for adults and focuses on awareness, personal resources, emotion management, boundaries, relationships, and change processes.

In the presence of significant symptoms, risks to oneself or others, or conditions requiring clinical evaluation, it is necessary to contact your doctor, psychologist, or psychotherapist.
