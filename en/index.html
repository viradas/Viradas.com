VIRADAS Method

VIRADAS is a method of orientation and accompaniment to change, developed to work with experiences of pressure in relationships and everyday life.

It helps to recognize where relationships, roles, or habits have become tight or suffocating and to create the conditions to regain pleasure and space, without forcing, but with gentle firmness.

VIRADAS is based on a clear and flexible reference structure, used to (re)read situations, reduce pressure, and restore room for choice.

Approach

The VIRADAS method integrates tools for listening and awareness inspired by Gestalt psychology, motivational interviewing, Nonviolent Communication (NVC), and references from traditional Chinese medicine.

It operates on the level of prevention, support, and development of personal resources and does not replace medical or psychotherapeutic paths.

How VIRADAS Was Born

Viradas was born from encounters with people and life moments marked by the feeling of living in a restricted space, among roles, expectations, or contexts perceived as having no way out.

This experience of constraint, sometimes described in psychology as entrapment, when prolonged over time can compromise emotional and relational well-being.

Pressures may be external, linked to demanding life conditions or contexts that concretely limit freedom. At other times they are internal: rooted beliefs, fears that guide choices, reaction patterns built over time.

Through work experiences in different contexts — from banking to the Brazilian comunidade, from employment reintegration programs to the penal institution — it became clear that people do not remain stuck due to lack of will, but because when inner space narrows, change becomes difficult, if not impossible.

When even a small degree of inner freedom is recovered, even in difficult contexts, it becomes possible again to choose, to take responsibility, and to rebuild dignity.

Viradas arises from this awareness: in Portuguese, virar means “to turn, to change direction.” The term also recalls vir (from Latin, “human being”) and vis (from Latin, “force”), understood as the energy that is activated when body, mind, and emotions return to dialogue, allowing rooting, reorganization, and openness to new possibilities.

Counseling

Counseling is professional support that helps you understand what you are experiencing, recognize emotions and blocks, and regain clarity, direction, and the ability to choose.
Depending on the reference approach, it works with thoughts, emotions, body, and actions.
It is for people going through standstills, crises, or change, without the need for a medical or psychotherapeutic intervention.
It is not psychotherapy, it does not provide diagnoses, and it does not replace healthcare.
It takes place in a safe and confidential space, with shared goals and timing, to support concrete steps of change.

Those who hold the title of Counsellor SGfB are recognized members of the Swiss Association for Counselling and work in accordance with its quality standards and the professional code of ethics.

Differences between Counseling, Psychotherapy, Psychology, Coaching

Counseling can complement other paths when you need a space for integration and clarity.
Knowing the differences between approaches helps you better understand what kind of support you may need.
The table below offers an orientation summary.

Goals Clarify conflicts and train relational skills. Analyze psychological functioning. Treat psychological distress and clinical disorders. Achieve results and improve performance.

Counseling sessions

Virada Relationship

A path that helps you bring order to your experience, clarify needs and boundaries, and identify concrete, sustainable steps.

Who it’s for
For those experiencing confusion, blocks, relational difficulties, transitions, or the feeling of “not being able to move from where they are”.

How it works
Sessions take place in the studio or online, individually or in a group. The work follows the Viradas method: active listening, conscious dialogue, guided reflections, and tools to better read your patterns. When useful, small activities or practical exercises are proposed to observe everyday life and support concrete, sustainable changes.

Duration
Sessions: 60 minutes for individual sessions, 120 minutes for group sessions
Path: 3–10 sessions, to be agreed depending on the type of support (orientation, accompaniment, etc.).

Languages
Italian, Portuguese, Spanish, English, German and French.

Consultations on habits and lifestyle

Virada Body&Sense

A path dedicated to the relationship with the body, energy, habits, and pace of life.
We explore habits, routines, and how emotions and periods of change influence well-being.
It integrates body awareness tools, non-medical principles from naturopathy and traditional Chinese medicine, breathing, and observation of inner signals.
It is not therapy, nor a healthcare or complementary medicine intervention.

Who it’s for
For those who feel in a “cage” of habits that no longer work or experience—after ruling out medical causes—fatigue and fluctuating energy, tension, stress-sensitive digestion, emotional eating, and difficulty taking care of themselves and their body.

How it works
Sessions take place individually, in the studio or online. Attention is given to body signals, rhythms, and daily hygiene and nutrition habits (not dietary plans) and to how emotions influence all of this. The work integrates counseling tools, well-being education, and non-medical principles from naturopathy and traditional Chinese medicine, offered in a simple and respectful way.

Note on the approach
The path integrates well-being tools that bring together the wisdom of traditional Chinese medicine and naturopathy from an educational and preventive perspective, fully respecting each person’s freedom and beliefs.

Duration
Sessions: 90 minutes for the first session + 30–45 minutes for the second feedback session, 60 minutes for follow-up sessions.
Path: 2–4 sessions, to be agreed depending on the type of support (orientation, accompaniment, etc.).

Languages
Italian and Portuguese. Also available in Spanish, English, German and French upon request.

Training and workshops

Virada KitLab

Virada KitLab is the Viradas method space dedicated to practical tools, maps, and guided activities to work concretely on emotions and emotional regulation, boundaries and communication, relational styles, motivation, habits, stress management and transitions, and team collaboration.
Each session offers tools that can be used immediately in daily life or in the workplace.

Who it’s for
Designed for people who want to go deeper into a specific topic (boundaries, emotions, energy, communication, habits), are looking for operational clarity and immediate tools without committing to long sessions.
For companies, Virada KitLab supports burnout prevention, effective communication, conflict management, emotional regulation at work, and organizational well-being, with formats customizable to team needs.

How it works
Sessions can be individual (personalized focus), group-based (thematic workshops), or corporate (experiential training for teams).
Group sessions are preferably in person.
Each activity can combine training moments, guided exercises, experiential practices, light body micro-practices, reflection spaces, and real-life applications.

Duration
Workshops can last from 90–180 minutes up to full-day formats. Mini-sessions are structured in 2–3 sessions of 60–90 minutes.
Corporate programs are designed tailor-made, defining duration and format together.

Languages
Italian and Portuguese. Also available in Spanish, English, German and French upon request.

Marlene Masino


She accompanies individuals and groups through processes of change, through counseling and training.
Her work arises from encounters with situations in which roles, relationships, or life contexts generate pressure and reduce room for choice.


Mission, vision and values

Mission
To contribute to people’s well-being by supporting those who feel “in a cage” to recognize who they are, what they want, and what concrete steps they can take to live choices and relationships with greater pleasure and satisfaction.

Vision
Respectful relationships and life contexts where it is possible to move without constraints and destructive dynamics, staying true to oneself in one’s choices.

Values

  • freedom of choice and movement
  • pleasure and well-being in relationships
  • clear communication and boundaries

Professional experience

After her first years after university in online marketing in the banking sector, Marlene also changed course.
Training in counseling and a volunteer experience in 2009 oriented her work toward the helping relationship.

Since then, she has gained experience in contexts where choices are limited and change becomes necessary:

  • with adolescents in contexts of marginalization, poverty and violence in Brazil, for three months.
  • with unemployed people, for six and a half years.
  • with people facing the consequences of a criminal conviction, for over sixteen years.

The counseling offered through Viradas falls within the field of the helping relationship, prevention, and personal growth.

It is not psychotherapy, it is not a healthcare profession, it does not provide diagnoses, it does not perform medical or psychological treatments, and it does not replace therapeutic or psychiatric pathways.

The service is intended for adults and focuses on awareness, personal resources, emotion management, boundaries, relationships, and change processes.

In the presence of significant symptoms, risks to oneself or others, or conditions requiring clinical evaluation, it is necessary to contact your doctor, psychologist, or psychotherapist.
