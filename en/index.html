Viradas

Viradas is a space created to relieve the feeling of pressure in relationships and daily life.

The goal is to identify where and how life has become tight, and to regain freedom of movement. Not by forcing it, but with gentle firmness.

Sessoions may involve dialogue, observing body signals, habits and lifestyle, and specific mindful movement practices.

Change can be navigated through seven stages, to be explored and redefined together.

Approach
The work integrates different references and tools, operating in the areas of prevention, support and the development of personal resources.
  • counseling sessions with a Gestalt-oriented approach
  • active listening techniques inspired by motivational interviewing (MI) and nonviolent communication (NVC)
  • observation of habits and lifestyle, with references to naturopathy and traditional Chinese medicine
  • body awareness practices inspired by yoga

This work can complement other paths, without replacing medical, psychotherapeutic or complementary therapy interventions.

How Viradas was born

Viradas was born from encounters with people and life moments marked by the feeling of living in a restricted space, between roles, expectations or contexts perceived as having no way out.

This experience of constraint, sometimes described as entrapment, if prolonged over time can affect emotional and relational wellbeing.

Pressures can be external, linked to demanding life conditions or contexts that concretely limit freedom.
At other times they are internal: deeply rooted beliefs, fears that guide choices, and response patterns built over time.

It is not always a matter of willpower. When inner space becomes restricted, change becomes difficult, sometimes impossible.
When even a small part of inner freedom is restored, even in complex contexts, it becomes possible again to choose, take responsibility and rebuild dignity.

Viradas was born from this awareness.

The name derives from the Portuguese concept of virada, “turning point, change of course.”
Rooted in Latin virare (to turn, to veer), it resonates with the words vir (“human being”) and vis (“force”), the vital energy that is activated when body, mind, and emotions return to a state of dialogue, allowing one to become grounded, reorganized, and open to new possibilities.
The choice of the plural form, "Viradas", emphasizes that change is not a single linear path to be followed, but is instead nurtured by many small moments and movements of turning.

The symbol

The symbol represents a turning process: a change that does not destroy, but reorganizes what is already there to create space.

The presence at the center
A living figure, not defined by rigid roles, but grounded and open, maintaining a solid base while moving with softness.

The polarities
The two side points represent the opposing forces within us: desire and fear, duty and pleasure. Freedom does not arise from eliminating one part, but from bringing them into dialogue.

The transformation
Lines that once felt like bars become roots that nourish or wings that open, moving from a constricted space to one that expands.

The roots of movement
At the base, the two V’s recall vir (human being) and vis (force). Every turning point starts from the body and felt experience, then expands into greater awareness.

The safe space
The circle is a protective and permeable space, holding without closing, allowing exchange and change without losing oneself.

Useful information

What is counseling

It is professional support to gain clarity, orient yourself in choices and rediscover autonomy. It works on thoughts and behaviors to promote change in a safe and confidential space.

It is not psychotherapy, it does not provide diagnoses and it is not a substitute for healthcare.

Goals and timeframes are always shared.

Differences between Counseling, psychotherapy, psychology, coaching

Counseling can accompany other journeys when a space for integration and clarity is needed.
Knowing the differences between the various approaches helps to better understand what type of support is needed.
The table below provides an orienting summary.
Counseling Psychology Psychotherapy Coaching
Goals Promote awareness and change. Assessment and support. Healing and restructuring. Performance and results.
Specific competence Listening, awareness and resources. Assessment, testing, consultancy. Diagnosis and therapeutic treatment. Goals and performance development.
Professional training Counseling school (at least 3 years). Master's degree + qualification (at least 5 years). Master's degree + qualification + specialization (at least 9 years). Coaching courses or schools (a few months).
Journey duration Short – medium term. Variable. Medium – long term. Short, goal-oriented.
Scopes and limits
The proposed work falls within the scope of counseling, lifestyle and personal resource development.
Through individual journeys and group workshops, the method integrates dialogue with practices of body awareness and movement, with an orientation toward well-being and prevention.

Professional limits
The activity does not constitute a medical, psychotherapeutic, alternative medicine or complementary therapy intervention.
  • No diagnosis: No clinical diagnoses are made.
  • No prescription: No drugs, supplements or remedies are prescribed.
  • No substitution: It does not substitute healthcare or specialist treatments.
The proposed practices (counseling, yoga, habit observation) aim to foster autonomy and the management of daily pressure, and can accompany other professional journeys when necessary.
Professional recognition


Marlene is a Counsellor SGfB and operates in respect of its quality standards and the professional code of ethics of the Swiss Association for Counseling.

It integrates references to yoga practice in line with the ethical commitment of Yoga Alliance.

Refunds and insurance
Counseling and the proposed activities do not fall under healthcare services and are not recognized by Swiss health insurance funds (LaMal/LCA/ASCA/RME).

For this reason, sessions are at the person's expense.

    Habit and Lifestyle Consultation

    Virada Body&Sense

    A journey to connect body signals with daily life, gaining a better understanding of how habits and tensions influence your balance.

    Through motivational interviewing, we explore habits and life rhythms to build a realistic and sustainable change, supported by personalized wellness guidelines.

    References to naturopathy and TCM (Traditional Chinese Medicine) are used as symbolic interpretations to make sense of the relationship between body and stress, from a perspective of awareness.

    The journey may include advice on natural nutrition and lifestyle from a purely educational perspective, operating outside the medical field, alternative medicine, or complementary therapies.


    Who it’s for:
  • Those who feel their body signals deserve to be heard.
  • Those seeking more balance in daily habits.
  • In the presence of signals like: fatigue, tension, difficult digestion, or emotional eating.
  • Periods of overload or prolonged stress.
